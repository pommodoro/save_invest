In this experiment you will be asked to make choices in a number of different scenarios. At the end of the experiment one of these scenarios will be selected at random, and you will receive payment based on the choices you made in that scenario. It is therefore important that you think carefully about your choices in each scenario.

You will receive a $5 payment for your participation in cash today. All other payments will be made by electronic transfer (either venmo or paypal). You will be asked to provide your account details at the end of the experiment. The questions you will answer will involve choosing between payments, some of which will take place today and some in one month's time. Payments will take place in the evening of the specified day (so, for example, if you are asked a question which involves payment today, that payment will be sent to you this evening). In addition to the $5 in cash, you will also receive a $5 participation fee via electronic transfer today and a further $5 transferred to you in one month's time. Note this means that, regardless of the decisions you make in the experiment you will receive 2 electronic transfers, one today and one in 1 month's time.

In each scenario you will be given a certain amount of money (your "endowment"). In most questions you will have two decision to make regarding what to do with this money.

First you must decide how much of it to receive today, and how much to save for the future. Any amount that you choose to receive today will be added to the $5 transfer that will be sent to you today. What happens to the amount you save for the future is described below.

Second, if you decide to save money for the future, you must decide how divide this money up between two available assets, called asset A and asset B. These assets may pay out an amount of money in one month's time. At this point, any money owing to you will be added to the $5 portion of the show up fee to be transferred to you in 1 month's time.

One and only one of asset A or asset B will pay out money. At the end of the experiment the computer will randomly select one of the two assets to pay out. The probability of each asset paying money will vary from scenario to scenario. You will be informed of the exact probabilities in each scenario before you make your decision.

The amount of money that each asset pays out if it is selected for payment is determined by its rate of return. This is a number between 1 and 5 which tells you how much you get back for every dollar you invest in that asset (assuming that that asset is chosen for payment). For example if the rate of return for Asset A is 1.2, you will receive $1.20 for every dollar you invest in Asset A, assuming that A is chosen for payment. If asset B is the one that pays out then you would receive nothing from this investment.

Again the rate of return for each asset will vary from scenario to scenario. You will be told the rate of return before you make a decision.

In some questions, the probability of one of the assets paying off will be 100%, and so the other asset will pay off 0% of the time. In such cases you will only have to decide how much you would like to save. Any money you save will automatically be invested in the asset that pays 100%.

You will now be asked some questions that test your understanding of the experiment. You will not be able to proceed until you have correctly answered these questions.
